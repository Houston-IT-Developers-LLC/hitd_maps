## 📜 LOUISIANA LAW - WHAT THEY MUST PROVIDE

### La. R.S. 44:1 - Definition of Public Records
```
"Public records" means all books, records, writings, accounts, letters and
letter books, maps, drawings, photographs, cards, tapes, recordings, or other
documentary materials, regardless of physical form or characteristics, including
information stored in electronic data processing equipment...made or received
by any public body...in connection with the transaction of public business...
```

**Parcel data = "records...maps...information stored in electronic data processing equipment"**

### La. R.S. 44:3 - Right to Inspect and Copy
```
"Except as otherwise provided by law, all public records shall be open to
inspection and copying...Every person has a right to inspect, copy, or
reproduce or obtain a reproduction of any public record."
```

**You have an ABSOLUTE RIGHT to this data.**

### La. R.S. 44:4 - Exemptions
**Review of all exemptions**: NONE apply to parcel boundary data, owner names, addresses, or tax assessments.

Exemptions that DON'T apply to parcel data:
- ❌ Security/safety (doesn't apply to property records)
- ❌ Privacy (property ownership is public in Louisiana)
- ❌ Trade secrets (government data isn't proprietary)
- ❌ Ongoing investigation (not applicable)

**BOTTOM LINE: They have NO legal basis to withhold parcel data.**

### La. R.S. 44:32 - Response Requirements
```
"The custodian shall within three days...make such public record available
for inspection...or shall within that time respond in writing stating the
specific legal exception upon which the refusal is based..."
```

**3-day response required. No exceptions.**

### La. R.S. 44:32 - Fees
```
"The custodian may require payment of reasonable copying charges...The charge
shall not exceed the actual cost of copying or reproduction..."
```

**Fees allowed but must be "reasonable" and cost-based.**

For commercial use: They can charge actual costs including staff time for extraction, but cannot make it prohibitively expensive to discourage access.

### La. R.S. 44:35 - Enforcement
```
"Any person who is denied the right to inspect or copy a public record...may
institute proceedings for the issuance of a writ of mandamus...The court shall
award reasonable attorney fees and costs..."
```

**If they refuse without legal basis, you can sue and they pay your attorney fees.**
