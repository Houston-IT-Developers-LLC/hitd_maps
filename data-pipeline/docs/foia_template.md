# FOIA/Public Records Request Templates for Parcel Data

This guide provides templates and guidance for requesting parcel/cadastral GIS data from counties that don't have open data portals.

## Why FOIA Works

Property parcel data is **PUBLIC RECORD** in all 50 states. Counties are legally required to provide this data upon request. The Freedom of Information Act (federal) and state-level public records laws guarantee access.

## Generic FOIA Request Template

```
Subject: Public Records Request - GIS Parcel Data

Dear [County Assessor/GIS Department],

Pursuant to [State Public Records Law - see table below], I am requesting a copy of
the county's GIS parcel/cadastral dataset in digital format.

Specifically, I request:
- All property parcel boundaries (polygon geometry)
- Associated attributes: Parcel ID/APN, owner name, property address
- Data format: Shapefile (.shp) or Geodatabase (.gdb) preferred
  - If not available: GeoJSON, KML, or any GIS-compatible format

This data is for use in a mobile application that helps hunters identify property
boundaries and ownership for legal compliance while hunting.

Please advise of any fees associated with this request. I am prepared to pay
reasonable reproduction costs.

If any portion of this request is denied, please cite the specific legal exemption
and provide the non-exempt portions.

Thank you for your assistance.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
[Email Address]
[Phone Number]
[Mailing Address]
```

## Finding the Right Contact

1. **County Assessor's Office** - Primary source, maintains property records
2. **County GIS Department** - May have better digital formats
3. **Tax Collector's Office** - Sometimes maintains parcel data
4. **County Clerk** - Backup if others don't respond

### How to Find Contact Info

1. Search: `[County Name] [State] assessor GIS data`
2. Check county website for "GIS", "Maps", or "Property Search"
3. Look for "Open Data" or "Data Download" pages first
4. Call the main county number and ask for GIS department

## Tips for Success

### Do:
- Be specific about what you need (parcel polygons, specific attributes)
- Offer to pay reasonable reproduction costs upfront
- Mention your legitimate use case (hunting app, property research)
- Follow up politely after the statutory response time
- Thank them for their assistance

### Don't:
- Request "all data" - be specific
- Be confrontational or cite legal threats immediately
- Expect instant turnaround - government moves slowly
- Forget to include your contact information

## Typical Outcomes

| Outcome | Frequency | Next Steps |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| Free data provided | ~60% | Download and process |
| Fee quoted ($50-$200) | ~25% | Pay fee, receive data |
| Directed to online portal | ~10% | Download from portal |
| Denied (rare) | ~5% | Request specific exemption, appeal |

## Sample Follow-Up Email

```
Subject: Follow-Up: Public Records Request - GIS Parcel Data (Submitted [DATE])

Dear [Name/Department],

I am following up on my public records request for GIS parcel data submitted on
[DATE]. Per [State Public Records Law], the statutory response time of [X] days
has passed.

Could you please provide an update on the status of this request?

Thank you for your assistance.

[Your Name]
[Contact Info]
```

## Handling Denials

If your request is denied:

1. **Ask for the specific exemption** - They must cite the legal basis
2. **Request non-exempt portions** - Parcel boundaries are rarely exempt
3. **Appeal** - Most states have an appeals process
4. **Contact State Records Ombudsman** - Many states have one

### Common (Invalid) Excuses

| Excuse | Response |
|--------|----------|
| "We don't have digital data" | Ask for any available format |
| "It's copyrighted" | Government data is public domain |
| "It contains private info" | Request just boundaries and parcel IDs |
| "We don't do bulk exports" | Offer to pay for staff time |
| "Commercial use prohibited" | Public records can't have use restrictions |

## Cost Expectations

| Data Type | Typical Cost |
|-----------|--------------|
| Shapefile download | $0 - $50 |
| Full county GDB | $50 - $200 |
| Custom extract | $100 - $500 |
| Per-record fees | $0.10 - $0.25/parcel |

Counties cannot charge more than "actual cost" of reproduction.
